# About This Command: `/investment-banking:one-pager`

## What it does
Creates a **company one-pager** (also called a teaser or executive summary) — the first document an investment bank sends to prospective buyers or investors when running a sell-side M&A process or fundraise. Its purpose is to generate interest without disclosing the company's identity (hence "teaser") or sensitive details before an NDA is signed. A good one-pager communicates why a company is exciting in under two pages.

## How to invoke
```
/investment-banking:one-pager
```
No arguments required. The skill prompts you to describe the company. Useful inputs:
- Business description and core product
- Key metrics (ARR, growth rate, gross margin, NRR, customer count)
- Team background and differentiation
- Fundraise or M&A situation (amount, use of proceeds, or exit rationale)

The skill works for any company stage — from early-stage growth rounds to pre-IPO processes.

## What it produces
A structured one-pager with:
1. **Company overview** — founding, HQ, team, stage, situation
2. **Business & positioning** — product description, competitive differentiation, key growth drivers
3. **Key financial metrics** — ARR, growth, margins, NRR, unit economics (CAC, LTV, Rule of 40)
4. **Investment highlights** — 4–5 bullets articulating the most compelling aspects of the business
5. **Representative customers** — anonymised or named customer examples with ACV
6. **Market opportunity** — TAM sizing and growth drivers
7. **Comparable M&A transactions** — recent deals with multiples to anchor valuation expectations
8. **Process overview** — dual-path summary (growth round vs. M&A) with indicative valuation range

## Typical use cases
- Investment banking analysts preparing first-round marketing materials
- Founders preparing their own "teaser" for a fundraise or sale process
- PE/VC firms packaging a portfolio company for an exit process
- Understanding how IB positions a company to a strategic or financial buyer

## BUSINESS & POSITIONING

**Core Business**
RetailAI delivers an AI-powered demand forecasting and automated replenishment engine purpose-built for mid-market and enterprise retailers. The platform ingests POS data, supplier lead times, seasonality signals, and external demand indicators to generate SKU-level replenishment recommendations — reducing stockouts, markdowns, and working capital tied up in excess inventory.

**Key Products**
- **DemandIQ** — SKU-level demand forecasting engine (ML-driven, 94% accuracy at 12-week horizon)
- **AutoReplenish** — automated PO generation and supplier routing triggered by DemandIQ signals
- **RetailPulse Dashboard** — real-time inventory health scoring across all store locations and DCs

**Competitive Positioning**
- Purpose-built for retail (vs. generic supply chain platforms like SAP IBP, Blue Yonder)
- Mid-market sweet spot ($50M–$1B retailer revenue) underserved by enterprise incumbents
- 8-week implementation vs. 12–18 months for SAP/Oracle deployments
- API-first, integrates natively with Shopify, NetSuite, Manhattan Associates, and major ERPs

**Key Growth Drivers**
- Retail inventory optimization market at $5B+ TAM, growing 22% CAGR
- 80% of mid-market retailers still using spreadsheets or legacy ERP planning modules
- AI/ML accuracy gains vs. statistical forecasting create measurable, auditable ROI
- Land-and-expand motion: average customer adds 2.3 product modules within 18 months of go-live
