# About This Command: `/wealth-management:financial-plan`

## What it does
Generates a **comprehensive personal financial plan** — the kind a Certified Financial Planner (CFP) would prepare for a client. It covers every dimension of a household's financial life: net worth, cash flow, retirement readiness, college funding, asset allocation, insurance gaps, tax optimisation, and estate planning. The output is a structured, actionable document with a prioritised to-do list the client can act on immediately.

## How to invoke
```
/wealth-management:financial-plan
```
No arguments required. The skill will ask for (or you can provide upfront):
- Client ages and retirement target ages
- Household income (sources and amounts)
- Current assets (retirement accounts, savings, real estate, investments)
- Liabilities (mortgage balance, rate, other debt)
- Financial goals (retirement lifestyle, college funding, major purchases)
- Risk tolerance (conservative / moderate / aggressive)
- Current savings rate and any existing plans (529, HSA, etc.)

The plan scales to whatever detail you provide — a rough input produces a directional plan; detailed inputs produce a precise one.

## What it produces
A 10-section financial plan covering:
1. **Executive summary** — overall assessment and highest-priority gaps
2. **Net worth statement** — assets, liabilities, and net worth breakdown
3. **Cash flow analysis** — income, expenses, savings allocation, and surplus identification
4. **Retirement projections** — Bear/Base/Bull scenarios, Monte Carlo success rates, income bridge analysis
5. **College funding gap analysis** — projected costs, 529 funding targets, realistic contribution schedule
6. **Asset allocation recommendations** — by account type (retirement, 529, taxable, emergency fund)
7. **Insurance review** — life, disability, umbrella, and health gaps with coverage targets and cost estimates
8. **Tax optimisation strategies** — 401(k) maximisation, Roth conversions, HSA, tax-loss harvesting
9. **Estate planning priorities** — urgency-ranked documents (wills, POA, beneficiaries, trusts)
10. **Action plan** — 20-item prioritised checklist with owners, costs, and deadlines (30-day, 90-day, 6-month, annual)

## Typical use cases
- Financial advisors generating first-draft plans for client review
- Individuals wanting a structured self-assessment of their financial health
- Demonstrating the breadth of wealth management advisory services
- Teaching personal finance planning to students or new advisors
